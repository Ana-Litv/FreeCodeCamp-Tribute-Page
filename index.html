Sergei Rachmaninoff

Russian composer

Here's a timeline of Sergei Rachmaninoff's life:

  • 1873 - Born on April 1st in Semyonovo, Russia
  • 1885 - Begins piano and music theory studies at the age of 12
  • 1891 - Graduates from the Moscow Conservatory with honors in piano and composition
  • 1892 - Premieres his first piano concerto, which receives mixed reviews
  • 1897 - Debuts as a conductor with the Moscow Philharmonic
  • 1901 - Composes his Piano Concerto No. 2 in C minor, which becomes one of his most famous works
  • 1902 - Marries Natalia Satina
  • 1904 - Completes his Symphony No. 2 in E minor, earning critical acclaim
  • 1906 - Experiences the first signs of a creative slump
  • 1907 - Embarks on a tour of the United States as a pianist
  • 1910 - Settles in Dresden, Germany
  • 1915 - Completes his All-Night Vigil, a significant choral work
  • 1918 - Begins a successful career as a concert pianist in Europe and the United States
  • 1930 - Settles in the United States to escape the political situation in Europe
  • 1934 - Completes his Symphony No. 3 in A minor 1939 - Embarks on a tour of Australia and New Zealand
  • 1943 - Passes away on March 28th in Beverly Hills, California
Sergei Rachmaninoff's life and musical legacy continue to inspire generations of musicians and music lovers around the world. His compositions are celebrated for their emotional depth and technical brilliance.
